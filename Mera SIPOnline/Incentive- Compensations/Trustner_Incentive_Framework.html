Incentive Design Philosophy

This framework is designed following best practices from global Fortune 500 financial services firms including HDFC Group, ICICI Group, Bajaj Finserv, AIA Group, Prudential Financial, and Charles Schwab. The model is built for a company preparing for public listing and ensures transparency, meritocracy, and shareholder accountability.

Meritocratic

Top performers earn 3-5x more in variable pay. Non-performers face structured consequences.

Profitable

Every incentive payout is tied to actual revenue. No payout without profit contribution.

Transparent

Every employee knows their earning potential from Day 1. System-driven, no manual overrides.

Key Principles (Global Best Practices Applied)

PrincipleDescriptionBenchmark Reference
Cost-to-Revenue RatioTotal employee cost (fixed + variable) must not exceed 35-40% of gross revenue for sales roles and 15-20% for support roles. This is the golden ratio used by profitable financial distribution firms globally.HDFC Life, Bajaj Finserv, AIA Group
Threshold-Based PayoutIncentives kick in only after achieving minimum 80% of target. Below 80% = zero incentive. This eliminates "participation trophy" culture.Prudential, MetLife, Charles Schwab
Accelerator Slabs101-110% = 1x multiplier, 111-130% = 1.15x, 131-150% = 1.25x, 150%+ = 1.5x. Super-performers get disproportionately rewarded.Tata AIA (your reference), ICICI Prudential
Trail Income = Incremental OnlyTrail sharing only on new/incremental business post assignment. No trail on legacy/inherited book unless fresh business is generated.Industry standard - NJ Wealth, IIFL Wealth
Profit Center AccountabilitySenior managers are evaluated as profit centers. Their incentive = f(vertical profit after ALL costs including attrition cost).Goldman Sachs, JP Morgan Wealth Mgmt
Hiring Cost AttributionCost of hires who leave within 6 months is attributed to the hiring manager's P&L. This discourages reckless hiring.McKinsey, Deloitte Financial Advisory
System-Driven CalculationAll incentive calculations are automatic. Only Super Admin (Ram Shah, Sangita Shah) can override. Deviation requires written approval from Directors.Listed company compliance requirement

Trail Income Sharing Model

The trail model applies to Mutual Fund AUM, Insurance Renewals, and other recurring commission products. This follows the NJ Wealth / IIFL Wealth model adapted for Trustner.

CRITICAL RULE: Trail sharing is ONLY on incremental business post-assignment. Legacy/inherited clients generate trail for the company, NOT the RM. If an RM is assigned existing clients and brings ZERO new business from them, trail share = 0%.

Hiring ROI & Cost Recovery Calculator

Before hiring any senior person or team, this calculator helps determine breakeven timeline and expected ROI. Follows the principle: every hire must have a business case.

Attrition Cost Attribution Rules

ScenarioCost AttributionImpact on Manager's P&L
Employee leaves within 3 months100% of salary paid attributed to hiring managerFull deduction from profit center
Employee leaves within 3-6 months50% of salary paid attributed to hiring managerHalf deduction from profit center
Employee leaves after 6 months (performing)No attribution - normal attritionNo impact
Employee leaves after 6 months (non-performing)25% attributed if target achievement < 50%Partial deduction
Quarterly Review Impact: When reviewing Q1/Q2/H1/Annual results, the attrition cost for each vertical is explicitly shown. If Vertical B recruited 10 people and 6 left within 6 months, the cost of those 6 salaries is added to Vertical B's cost. This creates pressure on managers to hire wisely and retain well.

Example: Your Kolkata Scenario

Scenario: Senior person at ₹1-3 lakh/month + team cost ₹5 lakh/month = ₹7 lakh/month total

6-Month Investment: ₹7L x 6 = ₹42 lakhs
12-Month Investment: ₹7L x 12 = ₹84 lakhs

Breakeven Requirement:
If average commission to company = 15-20% of business generated:
- To recover ₹42L in 6 months post grace = need ₹7L/month net revenue = ₹35-47L/month gross business
- To recover ₹84L in 12 months post grace = need ₹7L/month net revenue = ₹35-47L/month gross business

This person's team must collectively generate ₹35-50 lakh/month in premium/AUM to justify the cost.
