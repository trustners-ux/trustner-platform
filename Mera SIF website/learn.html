For distributors (MFDs)

The new SIF certification, explained — updated 23 July 2026.
Regulatory update · July 2026

One exam now covers both MF and SIF distribution.

By a SEBI circular dated 21 July 2026, the certification for distributing Specialized Investment Funds changed. The new NISM Series V-D — Mutual Fund & SIF Distributors Certification (live from 22 July 2026) qualifies a distributor for both mutual funds and SIFs with a single exam — no separate Series V-A required. The earlier two-exam route (Series V-A + Series XIII Common Derivatives) stops qualifying new SIF distributors after 21 September 2026.

Sources: SEBI circular "Certification Requirements for Distribution of Specialized Investment Funds (SIFs)", 21 July 2026 (sebi.gov.in) · NISM communique, 14 July 2026 (nism.ac.in).

Which certification do you need?

New distributor — MF + SIF
Series V-D alone. Per the circular, V-D holders "shall be eligible to distribute both Mutual Fund and SIF products" without separately holding Series V-A.
Mutual funds only
Series V-A continues unchanged — no sunset announced for the MF-only certification.
Already SIF-certified (V-A + Series XIII)
No re-exam needed. A valid Series XIII obtained on or before 21 Sept 2026 is grandfathered until it expires, provided a valid Series V-A is maintained. After 21 Sept 2026, Series XIII no longer qualifies new SIF distributors.

Syllabus: 22 chapters — 12 on mutual funds, 5 on equity derivatives, 5 on interest-rate derivatives (currency derivatives, part of the old Series XIII, is not included). NISM does not publish per-chapter weightage. Register at cert.nism.ac.in.

Why it matters

Only 8,371 distributors were AMFI-registered for SIFs at end-June 2026 (4,257 corporate, 4,115 individual — Cafemutual). A single, simpler exam opens SIF distribution to the far larger mutual-fund distributor base — which means wider access for investors and a bigger, better-served market.
