The one rule everything follows

We never publish a number we can't source.

The SIF category is new, and in a young market it is tempting to fill gaps with estimates. We don't. Every performance figure is computed from official data or attributed to a named source. Where a fund hasn't disclosed something yet — an AUM, an expense ratio — we show a dash, not a guess.

This is not just a style choice. Trustner is an AMFI-registered Mutual Fund & SIF distributor, and the same discipline that governs how we deal with clients governs what we publish: real, sourced, or clearly labelled — never fabricated.

How the site keeps itself current

MeraSIF updates on a schedule, automatically — not whenever someone remembers to. Five things run on their own:

Daily · ~6 AM IST
NAV engine
Fetches the day's official AMFI SIF NAVs, recomputes returns, and archives them for future window calculations.
Twice daily
News archive
Refreshes the SIF newsroom from public feeds, keeping a rolling, deduplicated archive of recent headlines.
Weekly · Monday
Industry update
Reviews the week's SIF developments and reconciles fund counts and timeline entries across the site.
Monthly · 15th
MeraSIF Monthly
Publishes a new edition of the newsletter — performance, launches, the regulatory record and one educational essay.
Continuous
New-fund detection
When a new SIF appears in AMFI's data, it's surfaced on the tracker automatically — no manual entry needed.

What we compute vs what we source

We compute the returns you can verify from public NAVs: since-inception (NAV against face value) and short-window changes (1-day, 5-day) from our archive of official daily NAVs. The arithmetic is point-to-point and absolute — no annualisation dressing-up, no smoothing.

We source the things only a data vendor or the fund house publishes: AUM, the riskometer band, the expense ratio, and — for now — the 1-month and 3-month returns, which come from Value Research until our own NAV archive is long enough to compute them from official data directly. Every sourced figure carries its origin and its as-of date on the page, so you always know how fresh it is.

What our data can't tell you (yet)

Honesty about the gaps is part of the standard. Here is where the data stops.

  • The category is young. India's first SIFs launched only from late 2025, so no fund yet has a one-year — let alone a multi-year — track record. Short histories can flatter or punish a strategy; treat every return here as an early data point, not a verdict.
  • Some funds haven't disclosed everything. The newest SIFs may not have published an AUM or expense ratio. We show "—" for those rather than fill them in.
  • Vendor data updates on its own schedule. AUM, risk band and TER come from Value Research and refresh when Value Research publishes, so they can trail the latest NAV. The as-of date is always shown.
  • Official NAVs can lag a day or two. AMFI occasionally publishes a fund's NAV with a short delay; we display the genuine as-of date rather than carry forward a stale one silently.
  • Risk bands are the AMC's, not ours. The 1–5 riskometer level is self-declared by each fund house under SEBI rules.
  • Holdings come only from official disclosures. SEBI requires SIFs to disclose portfolios periodically; we add holdings only when a fund actually publishes them.
  • The Trustner Fund Score is an opinion. It is our educational, qualitative view — not a SEBI-registered research rating and not a recommendation to buy or sell.
