How to read SIF returns

A young category needs the right lens. Three rules our desk applies before reading any SIF number:

1 · Read returns against the launch date
Most SIFs launched between Oct 2025 and Jun 2026, so a "since-inception" figure covers a different market window for each fund. A +6% SI from October is not comparable to +6% from March. We show the launch month next to every fund so the number has context.
2 · Judge a fund vs its benchmark, not the category average
During the early-2026 correction the gap between the best and worst Hybrid Long-Short SIF was several percentage points — category averages hide the real manager-quality gap. The honest comparison is each fund vs its own SEBI-disclosed benchmark over the same window.
3 · Cost and structure compound
Two SIFs with the same gross return diverge over time on TER and tax bucket. Check the TER column and run your own numbers in the tax calculator.
